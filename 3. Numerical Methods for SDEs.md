# Numerical Methods for Stochastic Differential Equations

## Motivation
SDEs model physical phenomena with uncertainty:
$$
dX_t = f(X_t, t) \, dt + g(X_t, t) \, dW_t
$$
- **Example**: Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) model:
  $$
  dB_t = (-K_1 B_t + s_t) \, dt + \sigma \, dW_t
  $$

---

## Mean Square Calculus
### Mean Square Limit
A sequence $X_n$ converges to $X$ in mean square if:
$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} E[(X_n - X)^2] = 0
$$
**Notation**: $\text{l.i.m.} \, X_n = X$.

**Properties**:
1. $\text{l.i.m.} \, aX_n = aX$.
2. $\text{l.i.m.} \, (X_n + Y_n) = X + Y$.

## ÃŽto Stochastic Integral
### Definition
$$
\int_{t_0}^t G_s \, dW_s = \text{l.i.m.} \sum G_{t_i} (W_{t_{i+1}} - W_{t_i})
$$
**Properties**:
- $E\left[\int G_s \, dW_s\right] = 0$.
- $E\left[\left(\int G_s \, dW_s\right)^2\right] = \int E[G_s^2] \, ds$.

---

## Stratonovich Integral
### Definition
$$
\int_{t_0}^t G_s \circ dW_s = \text{l.i.m.} \sum G_{\frac{t_i + t_{i+1}}{2}} (W_{t_{i+1}} - W_{t_i})
$$
**Difference**: Evaluation point is midpoint of interval.

---

## Numerical Schemes
### Euler Scheme
$$
X_{n+1} = X_n + f(X_n, t_n) \Delta t + g(X_n, t_n) \Delta W_n
$$
- **Strong order**: $O(\Delta t^{1/2})$.
- **Weak order**: $O(\Delta t)$.

### Milstein Scheme
$$
X_{n+1} = X_n + f \Delta t + g \Delta W_n + \frac{1}{2} g \frac{\partial g}{\partial x} (\Delta W_n^2 - \Delta t)
$$
- **Strong order**: $O(\Delta t)$ (scalar case).

---

## Fokker-Planck Equation
Describes evolution of probability density $p(x, t)$:
$$
\frac{\partial p}{\partial t} = -\sum \frac{\partial (f_i p)}{\partial x_i} + \frac{1}{2} \sum \sum \frac{\partial^2 ((g g^T)_{ij} p)}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}
$$

---

## Kernel Density Estimation
Estimate $p(x, t)$ from $N$ realizations:
$$
\hat{p}(x, t) = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^N K\left(\frac{x - X_t^n}{\lambda}\right)
$$
- **Bandwidth**: $\lambda_{\text{opt}} = O(N^{-1/(d+4)})$.
